<p>Virtue is like a rich stone, best plain set; and surely virtue is best in a body that is <span class="vocab-tip" data-tip="comely：標緻的、合宜的">comely</span>, though not of delicate features; and that hath rather dignity of presence, than beauty of aspect. Rejoice that you are beautiful, but let it not make you proud. For beauty is as summer fruits, which are easy to <span class="vocab-tip" data-tip="mar：損壞、毀損">mar</span>, and quite contrary to virtue; for it is generally seen, that beautiful persons are not otherwise of great virtue; as if nature were rather busy not to err, than to excel; and therefore they prove accomplished, but not of great spirit; and for the most part, they study behavior rather than virtue.</p>
<p>In beauty, that of favor, is more than that of color; and that of decent and gracious motion, more than that of favor. There is no excellent beauty that hath not some strangeness in the proportion. A man cannot tell whether Apelles, or Albert Durer, were the more trifler; whereof the one would make a personage by geometrical proportions; the other, by taking the best parts out of divers faces, to make one excellent. Such personages, I think, would please nobody but the painter himself. Not but I think a painter may make a better face than ever was; but he must do it by a kind of felicity, and not by rule.</p>
<blockquote>
    "That is the best part of beauty, which a picture cannot express."
    <span class="zh-trans">（美最精髓的部分，是畫作無法表現出來的。）</span>
</blockquote>
<p>If it be true that the principal part of beauty is in decent motion, certainly it is no marvel though persons in years seem many times more amiable; for no youth can be comely but by pardon, and considering the youth as to make up the comeliness. Beauty is as the summer fruits, which are easy to mar, and keep not. If virtue be accompanied with beauty, it makes virtues shine, and vices blush.</p>
