<p>I have been studying the traits and dispositions of the <span class="vocab-tip" data-tip="traits and dispositions：性格特徵與傾向">traits and dispositions</span> of the lower animals (so-called), and contrasting them with the traits of man. I find the result <span class="vocab-tip" data-tip="humiliating：使人感到羞辱、難堪的">humiliating</span> to me. Since it now seems plain to me that the theory ought to be vacated in favor of a new and truer one, this new theory to be named the Descent of Man from the Higher Animals.</p>

<blockquote>
    "Man is the only animal that blushes. Or needs to."<br>
    <span style="font-size:14px; font-style:normal; color:#8c8a88;">（人類是唯一會臉紅的動物，或者是唯一需要臉紅的動物。）</span>
</blockquote>

<p>In the course of my experiments, I chose a cage containing an old and ferocious Bengal tiger, and another containing a tame and gentle lamb. I introduced a man into the tiger's cage. The tiger immediately tore the man to pieces and ate him. I then introduced a man into the lamb's cage. The lamb did not hurt the man; indeed, the lamb welcomed him and tried to be friends. This proves that the lamb is vastly superior in moral disposition to the tiger, and that man is completely helpless in both situations.</p>

<p>Man is the only animal that harbors insults and injuries, broods over them, and takes his revenge when the opportunity offers. The higher animals have no such property. Man is the only animal that robs his helpless fellow of his country and takes possession of it; and drives him out of it or destroys him. He is the only animal that gathers his brethren around him and goes forth in cold blood to exterminate his kind. He is the only animal that inflicts pain for the pleasure of inflicting it.</p>

<p>Indisputably, Man is the only animal that holds property. Every other animal is content with what it can consume at the moment, but Man <span class="vocab-tip" data-tip="hoards：囤積、私藏">hoards</span> and steals, denying them to his starving neighbor. I have checked my findings carefully, and I am forced to conclude that Man is not the top of the evolutionary ladder, but rather the lowest, most defective animal ever produced by Mother Nature.</p>
